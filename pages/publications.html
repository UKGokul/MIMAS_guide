Publications & References

Key scientific publications underlying the MIMAS model and its applications. Papers are grouped by how they contribute to the model framework.

Core model framework & particle environment

  • Berger, U. & von Zahn, U. (2002)
    Icy particles in the summer mesopause region: Three-dimensional modeling of their environment and two-dimensional modeling of their transport
    Journal of Geophysical Research
    Foundational paper for the MIMAS particle–environment coupling, transport concepts, and Lagrangian treatment of ice particles.
  • von Zahn, U. & Berger, U. (2003)
    Persistent ice cloud in the midsummer upper mesosphere at high latitudes: Three-dimensional modeling and cloud interactions with ambient water vapor
    Journal of Geophysical Research
    Establishes long-lived mesospheric ice cloud behavior and coupling to background H₂O.

Microphysics & thermodynamics

  • Murphy, D. M. & Koop, T. (2005)
    Review of the vapour pressures of ice and supercooled water for atmospheric applications
    Quarterly Journal of the Royal Meteorological Society
    Provides the saturation vapour pressure formulation used in sub_tabelle for ice growth calculations.

Transport schemes & numerical methods

  • Walcek, C. J. (2000)
    Minor flux adjustment near mixing ratio extremes for simplified yet highly accurate monotonic calculation of tracer advection
    Journal of Geophysical Research
    Basis for the monotonic Eulerian advection scheme implemented in sub_transwalcek.

Note on terminology

The term MIMAS is not always explicitly stated in publications. Many studies listed here rely on the same modeling framework, physics modules, and numerical infrastructure developed and maintained at IAP Kühlungsborn.
